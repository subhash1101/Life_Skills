##  Question 1: What is the Feynman Technique?

The Feynman Technique tells that if you cant explain simply then you dont understand well enough. 
It means if one cannot explain the topic he/she learned in simple terms then it means that he/she didn't understand the topic well enough.

##  Question 2: Most Interesting Story or Idea

- The most interesting idea for me is the technique of learning.
- The technique of learning consists of two modes — focus mode and diffuse mode.
- Focus mode is the state that we are in when we are doing or learning something.
- Diffuse mode is the state that we are in when we are relaxing.
- Dali and Edison both use this technique; when they are out of ideas, they tend to relax themselves (into the diffuse mode), which results in the generation of new thoughts and ideas.

---

##  Question 3: Active and Diffused Modes of Thinking

- Active (Focused) mode means concentrating deeply on one problem or topic.  
- It is used when solving known problems or studying carefully.  
- Diffused mode happens when your mind is relaxed.  
- It helps you see big ideas and think creatively.  
- Learning works best when switching between focused and relaxed thinking.  
- Example: Study first, then take a break or walk.

---

##  Question 4: Steps to Approach a New Topic

- Break the topic into small simple parts.  
- Decide what you want to learn or achieve.  
- Learn basic concepts from a few good resources.  
- Remove distractions while practicing.  
- Practice regularly on important parts first.  
- Practice for at least 20 hours to overcome beginner frustration.

---

##  Question 5: Actions to Improve Learning Process

- Work with full focus and avoid distractions.  
- Understand concepts clearly and explain them in your own words.  
- Practice coding regularly and build projects.  
- Manage time well and follow deadlines.  
- Keep learning fun through curiosity and experimentation.  
- Ask questions and communicate problems early.
